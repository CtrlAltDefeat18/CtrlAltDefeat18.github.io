Empowering Underserved Communities Through Digital Equity

Who We Are

Vision: To create a future where digital equity empowers underserved youth and communities in rural and township areas.

Mission: To bridge the digital divide through AI-driven education, digital skills training, and community-driven initiatives.

What We Do

  • Cyber Awareness & Online Safety
  • Offline-first Learning Platform via Raspberry Pi
  • Freemium Training (TBC)
  • Community Partnerships (e.g., RUCE)

Our Impact

  • Raise cyber awareness for mature adults — target: 50 by Sept 2025
  • Pilot offline-first platform with 10–20 learners from Makhanda schools
  • Tutor & mentor Grade 8–11 learners in business skills, entrepreneurship, career guidance

Our Team

Aphelele Mjobo

Co-Founder & Technical Lead

Aphelele Mjobo is an IT: Systems Development student at the EBL Institute of Technology and a postgraduate student in enterprise management at Rhodes University, driven by a mission to build scalable, offline-first digital education tools for underserved communities. He co-founded CTG to merge education, social equity, and technology, ensuring that even the most remote learners have access to quality academic resources. With a background in community engagement and law, Aphelele leads CTG’s platform development, registration processes, and strategic direction. He is currently sharpening his data analysis skills through R programming on DataCamp and exploring the use of AI tools to enhance learner support across CTG’s platform. Outside of CTG, Aphelele enjoys gaming and reading spiritual texts on Zen, balance, and intentional living. He believes in the power of design and data to drive change, guided by the principle: "Artificial intelligence is not the future. It’s the present — we just haven’t caught up yet.”

Luthando David

Co-Founder

Luthando David is a Bachelor of Arts graduate in Legal Theory and History from Rhodes University, currently furthering their studies through a Postgraduate Diploma in Enterprise Management. Passionate about youth empowerment, education equity, and ethical leadership, Luthando is committed to building inclusive systems that prioritise people, potential, and purpose. Unathi Gongxeka

Co-Founder & Financial Manager

Unathi is currently pursuing a Postgraduate Diploma in Enterprise Management at Rhodes University, she brings a blend of academic insight and real-world experience to her evolving professional journey. Before returning to academia, she worked as a Junior HR Business Partner at Standard Bank Group, where she developed a strong foundation in talent management. This transition marks her second academic chapter at Rhodes University, having previously earned a Bachelor of Social Sciences Honours degree in Politics and International Studies. Stepping away from the corporate has allowed her to reflect deeply on the kind of impactful work she would like to be part of. This period of introspection led her to explore roles that align with purpose-driven impact, such as career guidance for high school scholars, where she can blend her HR background with a passion for youth development. I admire its inclusive approach to empowering underserved communities—both in rural and urban settings—by ensuring access to the tools, knowledge, and opportunities needed to participate meaningfully in the digital economy.
